Yes, absolutely. In fact, if you do not include a Privacy Policy in specific locations, **Apple and Google will reject your app submission.**

Since your app handles health data (Sensitive Personal Data), the scrutiny is much higher than for a game or a calculator.

Here is exactly where the Privacy Policy and "About" info must appear, and why the "Sign-up Consent" alone is not enough.

### **1. The Mandatory Locations (Where it MUST exist)**

You need to place your Privacy Policy in **three** distinct places:

#### **A. The App Store Listing (Pre-Download)**

* **Requirement:** Both the Apple App Store and Google Play Console require you to paste a **URL** to your privacy policy in the metadata section.
* **Why:** Users must be able to read how their data is handled *before* they decide to download the app.
* **The "Nutrition Label":** Apple will ask you to fill out a questionnaire (App Privacy Details). You must honestly declare:
* *Data Used to Track You:* **None.**
* *Data Linked to You:* **Contact Info** (if you have accounts), **Health & Fitness** (if you sync to cloud).
* *Data Not Linked to You:* **Diagnostics** (crash logs).



#### **B. Inside the App (Post-Download)**

* **Requirement:** Users must be able to review the terms *at any time*, not just when they click "I Agree" 3 years ago during sign-up.
* **Standard Location:** `Settings`  `About`  `Privacy Policy` link.
* **Why:** Under GDPR and New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, users have a "Right to be Informed." If they forget what they agreed to, they must be able to check.

#### **C. The Sign-Up Flow (Consent)**

* **Status:** You are already doing this. **Keep doing it.** This is the "active consent" required for processing health data.
