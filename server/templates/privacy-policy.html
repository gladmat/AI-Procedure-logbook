Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Opus utilizes a local-first architecture. Core clinical records and media are encrypted and stored locally on your device. Our servers store account and profile information and support services such as terminology lookups and transactional email. We do not sell your data.

1. Introduction

Opus ("we," "our," or "the App") is a surgical documentation tool developed by Dr. Mateusz Gladysz in New Zealand. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our mobile application.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, the Australian Privacy Act 1988, and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable.

2. Information We Collect

Account Information

  • Email address (for account creation and authentication)
  • Professional information you provide (name, career stage, country of practice, medical council number)

Clinical Data (Stored Locally)

  • Surgical procedure records
  • Patient identifiers (stored only on your device)
  • Operation notes and clinical documentation
  • Intraoperative photos (encrypted and stored on your device only)

3. How We Process Your Data

Local-First Architecture

Opus is designed with privacy as a core principle:

  • On-Device Encryption: Clinical records saved in the app are encrypted on your device using XChaCha20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption before local storage. A random device key is generated and stored using the secure storage available on your platform; it is not transmitted to our servers.
  • Patient Identifier Protection: Patient identifiers used for local indexing are protected before storage. Current devices use a per-user HMAC-SHA256 key on supported platforms, and older local records may retain a legacy SHA-256 hash until migrated locally.
  • Local-First Clinical Storage: Core case records and encrypted media remain on your device during normal app use. We do not use external AI services or cloud OCR on your clinical records.
  • Supporting Services: Some supporting functions involve our backend and third parties, including account/profile management, terminology lookup requests routed through our API, and transactional email delivery.
  • Media Encryption: Intraoperative photos are encrypted with the same authenticated encryption and stored on your device. They are never uploaded to our servers.

4. Data Storage and Security

  • Clinical data is stored locally on your device using encrypted storage
  • Account data is stored on secure servers in compliance with applicable data protection laws
  • We use industry-standard encryption for data transmission (TLS 1.3)
  • Access to backend systems is strictly controlled and audited

5. Your Rights

Under applicable privacy laws, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your account and associated data (available in Settings within the app)
  • Export your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise these rights, please contact us at support@drgladysz.com.

6. Data Retention

We retain your account information for as long as your account is active. Clinical data stored locally on your device is retained until you choose to delete it. You can clear all local data at any time through the app settings.

7. Third-Party Services

We use minimal third-party services:

  • Hosting and Authentication: Our backend provides account, profile, and authentication services.
  • Terminology Search: SNOMED CT lookup queries are proxied through our backend to Ontoserver.
  • Transactional Email: Password reset and onboarding emails are delivered through Resend.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any material changes through the app or via email.
